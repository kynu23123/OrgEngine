Current & Ongoing

The real state of our food system.

From labor exploitation to environmental devastation, our food supply has systemic problems that affect every person on the planet. Sources: Oxford Future of Food, Earth.org

Labor & Ethics

  • Sourcing produce from farms that exploit child labor and other vulnerable populations
  • Widespread unethical sourcing with little to no regulatory enforcement
  • Companies routinely conceal labor and production practices from consumers

Environmental Damage

  • Over-fishing, soil erosion, and water shortages destabilize food production globally
  • Effects compounded sharply by climate change
  • The EPA estimates ~¾ of factory farms discharge waste into waterways, yet fewer than ⅓ hold permits
  • Non-environmentally friendly vendors use excessive plastic packaging and generate substantial greenhouse gas emissions

Greenwashing Alert: Major corporations like ExxonMobil and Tyson Foods have faced legal action for deceptive "net zero" and "climate-smart" claims that misrepresent their actual environmental impact. Tyson lawsuit details

Food Insecurity & Health

  • Urban areas lack quality produce and lean toward processed foods that damage long-term health
  • Around 2 billion people globally lack micronutrients such as iron, zinc, vitamin A, and iodine
  • Food deserts contribute to the rise of chronic diseases in densely populated urban areas
  • More than half of U.S. adults — roughly 129 million people — live with one or more diet-related chronic diseases

Chemical Use & Transparency

  • Widespread use of pesticides and chemical enhancers on food crops
  • These chemicals are linked to harmful diseases in nearby communities
  • Companies are typically not transparent about their chemical practices

Supplier Sourcing

Who's really feeding America?

The food supply chain is dominated by a handful of powerful corporations — and their practices have far-reaching consequences for workers, communities, and the environment. Sources: Perfect Union, Eat This

Sysco

America's restaurant monopoly.

Sysco is a merger of nine food suppliers that has acquired over 150 companies, establishing a near-monopoly over the American restaurant supply industry. As its power grows, so does the harm to producers, employees, and consumers.

Key Context

  • Since 1980, Sysco has pushed to deregulate the trucking industry, reducing wages by ~40%
  • During the pandemic, Sysco passed inflation rates directly to customers while its profits surged 139%
  • Its failed bid to acquire U.S. Foods was blocked, but 3rd and 4th largest suppliers are planning their own megamerger
→ Read full report

Documented Concerns

  • Formerly partnered with Chinese producers accused of using forced labor
  • Purchases berries from Driscoll's, accused of widespread wage theft
  • Purchases meat from Tyson Foods, accused of child labor violations, animal cruelty, and illegally restricting meat supply
  • Aggressive acquisitions of competitors reduce choice for restaurants and drive down quality
  • Ongoing leveraging of market power over smaller producers and workers

Cargill

The U.S.'s largest privately-owned company

Cargill supplies everything from meat to chocolate across the globe. Its enormous scale allows it to routinely violate regulations and cause environmental destruction across multiple continents.

Scale of Operations

  • Responsible for one-third of all cacao (chocolate) supply worldwide
  • Major supplier of meat, soy, and commodity crops across the Americas and Africa
  • Domestic operations responsible for thousands of pounds of air and water contaminants annually
Compare ethical sourcing

Documented Concerns

  • Notorious for bacterial outbreaks due to contaminated meat products
  • Pushed expansion of meat farms into South America, destroying millions of protected Amazon rainforest acres
  • Harmful soy production encroaches on Native American and protected lands
  • Use of harmful pesticides near villages has killed numerous Native American children
  • African cacao operations have destroyed millions of trees and harmed endangered species including Elephants and Chimpanzees
  • Knowingly purchased cacao harvested by child slaves and provided training to those vendors

Ethical Alternatives

Sourcing that puts people and the planet first.

B Corps and certified ethical suppliers offer a different model — one that prioritizes worker welfare, environmental responsibility, and transparency.

What to Look For

  • B Corporation certification, verified social and environmental standards
  • Direct-from-farm sourcing with transparent supply chains
  • Fair trade certifications for cacao, coffee, and produce
  • USDA Organic and Animal Welfare Approved labels

Chipotle Beer & Butter Shrimp

Gluten-Free Option Dairy-Free Option High Protein

A smoky, spiced shrimp foil pack cooked on the grill , bold flavors with minimal cleanup. Food Network

Ingredients

  • 1¼ lbs peeled & deveined large shrimp
  • 5 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2 tsp chipotle hot sauce (plus more to taste)
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ¼ tsp ground coriander
  • ¾ tsp kosher salt
  • ½ cup Mexican-style lager (e.g. Corona)
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 1 lime, halved
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 cups cooked white rice

Steps

  1. Prepare grill for medium heat. Lay out a 12×18" piece of heavy-duty foil, fold the four sides up to create walls, and spread the shrimp in the center. Toss with garlic, chipotle sauce, cumin, oregano, coriander, and salt. Pour in beer and dot with butter. Cover with another sheet of foil and crimp edges tightly.
  2. Transfer foil pack to grill, close the lid, and cook until shrimp are pink and cooked through, 4–6 minutes. Remove from heat and rest a few minutes. Carefully tear open , watch the steam. Squeeze lime juice over shrimp, sprinkle with cilantro, and serve with rice.

Healthier Honey Garlic Chicken

Dairy-Free Gluten-Free Option High Protein

Crispy, caramelized chicken thighs in a sweet-savory honey garlic glaze. Foodess

Ingredients

  • 1 lb chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 4 tbsp cornstarch
  • ⅓ tsp kosher salt
  • 3 tbsp avocado oil (or other high smoke point oil)
  • 4 tbsp honey
  • ⅓ cup chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp minced garlic
  • 2 green onions, sliced

Steps

  1. Toss chicken pieces with cornstarch and salt to coat evenly. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat until shimmering. Add chicken in a single layer and cook undisturbed until deeply golden on the bottom. Flip and cook another 2 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, whisk together honey, chicken broth, and soy sauce and set aside.
  3. Push chicken to one side of the skillet. Cook garlic in the empty space until soft, about 2 minutes. Add honey mixture, stir through chicken to coat. Simmer until thickened, 2–3 minutes. Chicken should reach 165°F. Top with green onions.
Breakfast

Gluten-Free Egg Bites

Gluten-Free High Protein Meal Prep

Fluffy, customizable egg bites made in a muffin pan with a steam bath for the perfect texture.

Ingredients

  • 8 eggs
  • Cottage cheese (to taste)
  • Pork or turkey bacon (optional)
  • Shredded cheese (optional)
  • Pinch of salt
  • Black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Add eggs, cottage cheese, a pinch of salt, and black pepper into a high-powered blender. Blend until smooth.
  2. In a muffin pan on a baking sheet, add your choice of meat and cheese to each cup.
  3. Pour the blended egg mixture over the fillings.
  4. Create a water bath: place a baking pan on the bottom rack of the oven, filled halfway with water.
  5. Place the egg bites in the oven at 325°F for 20–25 minutes. Done!
Breakfast

Dairy-Free French Toast

Dairy-Free Vegetarian Quick

Golden, cinnamon-spiced French toast using almond or oat milk — just as indulgent, dairy free.

Ingredients

  • Sturdy bread of your choice
  • Almond milk or any non-dairy milk
  • Eggs
  • Vanilla extract
  • Cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Add eggs, almond milk, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and a pinch of salt into a shallow bowl. Whisk until smooth and combined.
  2. Heat your skillet to medium heat. Ensure it is fully heated before adding bread.
  3. Place bread into the shallow bowl with the mixture and let it soak for 1 minute per side.
  4. Cook on the skillet for 2–3 minutes on each side until golden brown and cooked through. Done!

Past Food Issues

We've been here before.

History shows a clear pattern: industry concealment, public harm, legal battles, and eventual transparency. We are in the middle of this cycle again — right now.

E. Coli Outbreaks in the U.S.

Recurring E. Coli outbreaks linked to contaminated meat and produce exposed major failures in food safety oversight. Companies like Cargill faced repeated scrutiny for contaminated beef products tied to consumer illness.

The Tobacco Industry Playbook

For decades, tobacco companies refused to acknowledge the lethal health impacts of cigarettes. Extensive legal battles — culminating in the Master Settlement Agreement — finally forced disclosure. Restrictions on tobacco products are now far stronger than in the 20th century. The food industry is following a nearly identical playbook today. MSA details

The Pattern Repeats

Today, companies like Tyson Foods and Cargill engage in greenwashing, labor violations, and supply chain concealment — mirroring the tactics of the tobacco era. OrgEngine exists because this time, we don't have to wait decades for the truth.

The Lesson of Tobacco

It took a generation of legal battles and advocacy to force the tobacco industry to admit its products caused cancer. The food industry's deceptive practices are on a similar trajectory — but with the right information, consumers can accelerate accountability.
